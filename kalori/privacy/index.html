Kalori Privacy Policy

Effective 7 July 2026

Kalori: Calorie Tracker ("Kalori", "we") is made by Dahl Industries AB. Kalori is built to work entirely on your iPhone. It understands what you eat, reads your photos, and estimates calories using Apple's on-device intelligence — so your food, photos, and health information never leave your device. There are no accounts, no ads, and no third-party tracking or analytics.

Everything stays on your device

Your whole food journal — meals, photos, calories and macros, exercise, water, weigh-ins, your profile details, and your daily goal — is stored only on your iPhone. We have no server that holds a copy, and we cannot see your entries.

The intelligence runs on-device too. When you photograph a meal, type or dictate what you ate, Kalori analyses it using Apple's built-in on-device model and Apple's Vision and Speech frameworks. None of that content — no photos, no meal descriptions, no calorie or health data — is sent to us or to any third party.

The one thing that leaves the device: barcode lookups

If you scan a product barcode while you are online, Kalori sends only the barcode number to the free Open Food Facts database to fetch that product's public nutrition facts. This request contains no personal information — no name, no account, no identifiers, no location — just the product's number, exactly like looking a product up in a public catalogue. If you are offline, Kalori reads the package with the on-device camera instead, and nothing is sent at all.

Camera, microphone & photos

Kalori asks for camera access to photograph meals and scan barcodes, microphone and speech-recognition access to let you log by voice, and photo-library access if you choose an existing photo. These are used only for the action you take, and the results stay on your device. Voice dictation is transcribed by Apple's on-device speech recognition.

No accounts, ads, or tracking

  • Kalori has no sign-up and no user accounts.
  • Kalori shows no advertising.
  • Kalori includes no third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs, and does not track you across apps or websites.

Deleting your data

Because your journal is stored on your device, deleting the Kalori app removes all of your entries and profile data. There is nothing further for us to delete on our side.

Children

Kalori is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles data, we will update this page and the effective date above.
