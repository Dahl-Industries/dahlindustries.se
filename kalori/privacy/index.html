Kalori Privacy Policy

Effective 4 July 2026

Kalori: Calorie Tracker ("Kalori", "we") is made by Dahl Industries AB. We built Kalori to be private by default: there are no accounts, no ads, and no third-party tracking or analytics. This policy explains what stays on your iPhone and the limited data that is sent out to make the app work.

What is stored on your device

Your food journal lives on your iPhone. Everything you log — meals, photos, calories and macros, exercise, water, weigh-ins, your profile details, and your daily goal — is stored locally on the device using Apple's on-device storage. We do not have a server that keeps a copy of your journal, and we cannot see your entries.

What is sent out, and why

To understand what you ate, Kalori sends the content you choose to log to trusted service providers, only when you take that action:

  • Food analysis and imagery (Google). When you log by photo, voice, or text, that meal photo or description is sent to Google's Gemini API to identify the food, estimate its calories and macros, and generate the illustrative dish images shown in your journal. This processing is subject to Google's terms and privacy practices. Google states that data sent through the Gemini API is not used to train its models.
  • Barcode lookups (Open Food Facts). When you scan a barcode, the barcode number is sent to the Open Food Facts database to retrieve that product's nutrition facts. No personal information is included in this request.

These requests carry only the item you are logging. They do not include your name, contacts, or location, and Kalori does not attach an advertising identifier to them.

Camera, microphone & photos

Kalori asks for camera access to photograph meals and scan barcodes, microphone and speech recognition access to let you log by voice, and photo-library access if you choose an existing photo. These are used only for the action you take in the app. Speech you dictate is converted to text using Apple's speech recognition on your device or through Apple's service, as provided by iOS.

No accounts, ads, or tracking

  • Kalori has no sign-up and no user accounts.
  • Kalori shows no advertising.
  • Kalori includes no third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs, and does not track you across apps or websites.

Deleting your data

Because your journal is stored on your device, deleting the Kalori app removes all of your entries and profile data. There is nothing further for us to delete on our side.

Children

Kalori is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles data, we will update this page and the effective date above.
