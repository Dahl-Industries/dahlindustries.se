Kalori: Calorie Tracker — iPhone, iOS 26 or later

Kalori is a food journal that listens. Instead of searching endless databases, you photograph your plate, say what you ate, or scan a barcode — and Kalori estimates the calories and macros and logs them for you. Here's how to get the most from it.

Logging your food

There are three ways, and you can mix them freely:

Photograph it. Tap the shutter and take a picture of your meal. Kalori reads the dish, estimates the calories, protein, carbs, and fat, and considers the time of day to sort it into breakfast, lunch, dinner, or a snack.

Say or type it. Tap the microphone or the text field and describe your meal in plain language — "a chicken caesar salad and a sourdough roll." Kalori logs each item in seconds.

Scan a barcode. Point the camera at a packaged food's barcode to pull its nutrition facts, then choose your serving and log it with a tap.

Exercise, water & weight

Tell Kalori "30 minute run" and it logs the workout and adds the burned calories back to your daily budget. You can also log water and record weigh-ins the same way — just say or type it.

Your daily goal

When you first open Kalori, a short setup calculates a personalized daily calorie goal from your body, activity level, and whether you want to lose, maintain, or gain weight. You can adjust your details anytime, and the goal updates with them.

The journal

Every entry becomes a studio photograph in your journal. Pinch or tap the handle to zoom out to a magazine-style catalog of your days, and swipe between days to review or add to any of them.

Recipes

Ask Kalori for a healthy meal and it shares a recipe with the calories and macros already worked out. Made it? Log a serving in one tap.

Languages

Kalori is available in 69 languages and follows your iPhone's language automatically. It even replies to you in your own language.
